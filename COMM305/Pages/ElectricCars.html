Electric Cars

Electric vehicles can help increase energy security, improve fuel economy, lower fuel costs, and reduce emissions.

Facts:

  • Electric cars are up to 80% efficient, compared to gasoline powered vehicles that are only 14-26% efficient.

  • The range of most electric cars is plenty for daily city needs.

  • While the purchase price of an electric vehicle may be higher than that of a conventional vehicle, this increase is often offset by fuel cost savings, federal tax credit, as well as state incentives.

  • The price of an EV is due to market factors, not cost. Therefore, the price will decline with favorable market conditions, such as more EV's on the road.

Benefits:

  • Electric cars emit no tailpipe pollutants.

  • The average consumer can save nearly $1,000 per year on gasoline by switching to an all eletric vehicle.

  • Electric cars require little maintenance compared to standard fuel powered cars.

  • Electric cars are very quiet and deliver a smoother ride than standard cars.
