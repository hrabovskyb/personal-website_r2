Water Conservation

Water conservation is something we all should practice. Water is a very important element in our lives. It's too precious to waste. Here are some useful facts and simple suggestions that will help you understand more about water. They'll help you save water without any great inconvenience.

Facts:

  • The average person wastes 10 gallons of water per day due to leaks.

  • A large amount of water is used to produce food:

    • It takes approximately 1,000 gallons per person per day to produce the average American diet.

    • A serving of poultry takes about 90 gallons of water to produce

    • A cup of coffee takes 55 gallon of water to make (most of which is used to grow the coffee beans)

  • A gallon of gasoline takes nearly 13 gallons of water to produce

  • Recycling a pound of paper saves about 3.5 gallons of water.

  • One drip one second from a leaky faucet wastes 540 gallons of water a year.

Tips:

  • Repair leaky faucets and toilets.

  • Intall new water-efficient fixtures, such as faucets and shower heads.

  • Take showers instead of baths.

  • Eat less meat and dairy to reduce your indirect water footprint.

  • Carpool or take public transportation (reduces both energy and water use!)

  • Buy recycled goods.

  • Recycle your old newspapers and old documents rather than throwing them away.

    • Better yet, go paperless to reduce paper production.
